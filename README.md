### Semaglutide (Compounded)

Semaglutide is a synthetic analog of the naturally occurring GLP-1 hormone. It mimics GLP-1's effects on the pancreas and brain, increasing insulin secretion, reducing glucagon, and signaling satiety to the hypothalamus.

Clinical research on semaglutide shows average body weight reductions of 10–15% in trials with overweight and obese adults.

Potential side effects include nausea, vomiting, and injection-site discomfort. Note: While the active ingredient in Ozempic/Wegovy is FDA-approved, the compounded versions are not individually FDA-approved products.

### Tirzepatide (Compounded)

Tirzepatide is a novel dual GLP-1 and GIP receptor agonist. Clinical data indicates superior average weight loss compared to semaglutide alone, clinical trials report average reductions of up to 20–22% of body weight.

It works through two complementary hormonal pathways, enhancing satiety and energy expenditure simultaneously. Potential side effects include gastrointestinal discomfort, constipation, and fatigue.
